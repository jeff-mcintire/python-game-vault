## Appearance

Lily is a slight girl with her mother's dark hair—though hers hangs loose around her shoulders rather than pinned back—and her father's warm brown skin. She has large, observant eyes that seem to be constantly taking everything in. Ink stains frequently mark her fingers, and she's almost always carrying a battered book tucked under one arm.

She wears simple homespun dresses with deep pockets where she stashes interesting things she finds: unusual stones, pressed flowers, scraps of paper with notes.

## Personality

**Traits:**
- Quiet and observant; often overlooked, which suits her fine
- Curious about everything, especially old stories and strange occurrences
- Kind-hearted but shy around strangers
- Has a surprisingly dry wit when she opens up

**Ideals:**
- *Knowledge* — Books hold answers to questions no one else thinks to ask
- *Quiet Courage* — You don't have to be loud to be brave

**Bonds:**
- Adores her father [[Gareth Hollowell]]'s stories of distant places
- Helps her mother [[Hanna Hollowell]] in the kitchen without complaint
- The few books the inn owns are her most treasured possessions
- Secretly wishes she could see the places in her father's tales

**Flaws:**
- So quiet she's sometimes forgotten or overlooked
- Gets lost in books and daydreams, neglecting her chores
- Asks questions that make adults uncomfortable
- Hides when overwhelmed rather than asking for help

## Background

Born and raised at [[The Hearthstone Inn]], Lily has spent her whole life surrounded by travelers' tales and village gossip. While her brother Tam dreams of adventure, Lily prefers to read about it—though lately, she's been reading stranger things.

She found an old book hidden in the inn's cellar last year, filled with stories about [[Willowmere]] that no one tells anymore. Since then, she's been quietly collecting odd tales and asking questions that make the older villagers exchange worried looks.

## Role at the Inn

Lily helps out wherever she's needed:
- Assists her mother [[Hanna Hollowell]] with food preparation and baking
- Clears tables and washes dishes during busy evenings
- Fetches supplies from [[Mabel's Sundries]]
- Brings meals up to guests in their rooms
- Keeps the firewood stocked by the great hearth

She's small and easily overlooked, which means she overhears *everything*.

## Roleplaying Notes

- Speaks softly; players might need to lean in to hear her
- Will warm up to anyone who shares books or interesting stories
- Watches strangers carefully from corners and doorways
- Might slip adventurers useful information if she likes them
- Ask her about the inn and she'll share surprisingly detailed observations

## Plot Hooks

- **The Old Book:** The book she found in the cellar contains unsettling local legends—and a map
- **Strange Questions:** Her recent behaviour worries her mother; she's been reading odd texts and asking about things best left alone
- **The Quiet Witness:** Lily sees and hears everything at the inn—she might know secrets guests think are private
- **Latent Gift?:** Her unusual interests might hint at a budding talent for magic or divination
- **Missing:** If Lily ever disappeared, the whole village would mobilize—and her parents would pay any price

## Secrets

- The book she found mentions something sleeping beneath the old willow tree at the village's heart
- She's been teaching herself to read an old script no one uses anymore
- Sometimes she dreams of places she's never been—places from her father's stories
