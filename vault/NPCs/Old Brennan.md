# Old Brennan

> *"The sheep know things. They knew the winter of '42 was coming weeks before we did. Best listen to the sheep."*

## Appearance

Brennan is a wizened old man, weathered by decades on the windswept hillsides. He's thin and wiry, with a face like cracked leather and surprisingly bright blue eyes. His white hair is wild and unkempt, and he walks with a gnarled staff that's as much companion as tool. He smells faintly of lanolin and woodsmoke.

## Personality

**Traits:**
- Eccentric; talks to his sheep (and claims they answer)
- Knows the hills and weather better than anyone
- Surprisingly spry for his age
- Speaks in cryptic observations and old sayings

**Ideals:**
- *Simplicity* — Life makes sense when you're tending sheep
- *Patience* — The hills teach you to wait

**Bonds:**
- His flock is his family; knows each sheep by name
- Has watched over [[Willowmere]] from the hills for fifty years

**Flaws:**
- Considered "touched" by some villagers
- Can be maddeningly vague when asked direct questions
- Forgets conversations happened, or claims to

## Background

Brennan has been the village shepherd for as long as anyone can remember. He never married, never wanted to—says the hills are his wife and the sheep are his children. He lives in a small stone hut on the hillside above [[Willowmere]], coming down only occasionally for supplies.

Some say he's gone a bit mad from solitude. Others say he's the wisest man in the valley. The truth might be somewhere between.

## Roleplaying Notes

- Speaks in a meandering way, going off on tangents
- References his sheep's "opinions" on matters
- Has actually seen strange things in the hills over the years
- More lucid than he appears; sometimes plays up his eccentricity

## Plot Hooks

- Has seen the strange lights in the mountains—repeatedly, and close
- May have glimpsed things others haven't from his hillside vantage
- His sheep have been nervous lately; he thinks something is coming
- Knows old paths through the mountains that aren't on any map
