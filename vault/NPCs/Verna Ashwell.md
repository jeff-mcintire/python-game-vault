# Verna Ashwell

> *"Every thread tells a story, dear. You just have to know how to read them."*

## Appearance

Verna is a thin, angular woman with sharp features and greying black hair pulled into a tight bun. Her fingers are long and nimble, always in motion—if not at her loom, then knitting or mending. She dresses in clothes of her own making, always impeccably neat, and wears small spectacles when doing fine work.

## Personality

**Traits:**
- Meticulous and detail-oriented
- Proud of her craft; accepts no shoddy work
- Quietly observant; notices small details others miss
- Traditional; preserves old patterns and techniques

**Ideals:**
- *Craft* — Quality work lasts generations
- *Tradition* — The old ways carry wisdom

**Bonds:**
- Her loom has been in her family for four generations
- Teaches [[Willowmere]]'s young women to weave and sew

**Flaws:**
- Can be judgmental of others' work (and lives)
- Holds grudges; never forgets a slight
- Tendency to gossip, though she calls it "observing"

## Background

Verna's family has woven cloth in [[Willowmere]] for generations. She learned from her mother, who learned from hers, preserving patterns and techniques that date back centuries. She's fiercely proud of this heritage and sees herself as its guardian.

She married Roderick, a farmer of few words who was happy to let her take charge. Their daughter Nan is her apprentice; their son Bram helps his father with the fields.
