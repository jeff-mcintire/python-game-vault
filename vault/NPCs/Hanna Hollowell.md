# Hanna Hollowell

> *"You'll eat what I serve and you'll be grateful for it. ...Would you like seconds?"*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Race** | Human |
| **Gender** | Female |

## Appearance

Hanna is a sturdy woman with capable hands and an expression that shifts between stern and amused in an instant. Her dark hair, streaked with grey, is always pulled back practically, and she wears a perpetually flour-dusted apron. Her eyes are sharp and miss nothing, especially when it comes to troublemakers in her establishment.

## Personality

**Traits:**
- Practical and no-nonsense; runs a tight kitchen
- Fiercely loving beneath a gruff exterior
- The disciplinarian of the family
- Will feed anyone who's hungry, no questions asked

**Ideals:**
- *Hard Work* — Nothing good comes without effort
- *Family* — Everything she does is for them

**Bonds:**
- [[The Hearthstone Inn]] is her domain
- Her husband [[Gareth Hollowell]] is the love of her life, even when he's being foolish
- Worries constantly about her children's futures

**Flaws:**
- Can be sharp-tongued when stressed
- Worries too much; tends to catastrophize
- Stubborn about admitting when she's wrong

## Background

Hanna was born and raised in [[Willowmere]]. When a wandering sailor stumbled into the village with wild stories and a wilder smile, she surprised everyone—including herself—by falling for him. She agreed to marry Gareth only after he promised to stay put.

She's the practical backbone of [[The Hearthstone Inn]], managing supplies, cooking meals, keeping accounts, and generally ensuring the place doesn't fall apart while her husband charms the customers.

## Roleplaying Notes

- Commands her kitchen like a general commands troops
- Softens visibly around children and anyone genuinely in need
- Will feed adventurers generously, then interrogate them about their intentions
- Her stew is legendary; villagers claim it can cure anything

- Knows village dynamics intimately; can advise on who to trust
- Her daughter Lily has been acting strangely—reading old books, asking odd questions
