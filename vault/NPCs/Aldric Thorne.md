# Aldric Thorne

> *"The land provides, so long as you respect it."*

## Basic Information

| | |
|---|---|
| **Race** | Human |
| **Gender** | Male |

## Appearance

Aldric is a tall, weathered man with a face like carved oak—deeply lined but strong. His hair has gone entirely silver, kept short and practical. His hands are large and calloused from a lifetime of working the soil, and he walks with a slight stoop from decades of labor. His eyes are a pale, watery blue that still hold a keen intelligence.

He dresses in simple homespun clothes, always wearing a wide-brimmed hat to shield against sun and rain alike.

## Personality

**Traits:**
- Calm and deliberate; thinks before speaking
- Commands respect through wisdom rather than authority
- Deeply traditional; values the old ways
- Protective of the village and its people

**Ideals:**
- *Community* — "We look after our own. That's how it's always been."
- *Stewardship* — The land is a trust to be passed to the next generation

**Bonds:**
- His family has farmed the same land for five generations
- Feels responsible for the wellbeing of all [[Willowmere]]

**Flaws:**
- Resistant to change; suspicious of new ideas
- Can be overbearing with his children and grandchildren

## Family — The Thornes

The Thorne family is one of the oldest in [[Willowmere]], owning the largest farm on the village's eastern edge.

## Background

Aldric has lived his entire life in [[Willowmere]]. He took over the family farm from his father and has watched the village through good years and lean. When disputes arise or decisions must be made, villagers naturally turn to him—not because of any official title, but because his judgment has proven sound time and again.

Since his wife Marta's death, he has thrown himself into work and village matters, rarely speaking of his grief.
