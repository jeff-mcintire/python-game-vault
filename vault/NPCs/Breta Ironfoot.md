# Breta Ironfoot

> *"Good steel doesn't lie. Neither do I."*

## Basic Information

| | |
|---|---|
| **Race** | Dwarf |
| **Gender** | Female |
| **Age** | ~180 years (middle-aged by dwarf standards) |
| **Occupation** | Blacksmith |
| **Location** | [[Ironfoot Smithy]], [[Willowmere]] |

## Appearance

Breta is stocky and powerfully built, even by dwarven standards. Her arms are thick as tree trunks, corded with muscle from decades at the forge. Her skin is weathered and ruddy, perpetually smudged with soot and ash no matter how recently she's washed. 

Her hair is a faded copper-red, streaked with grey, typically pulled back in a practical braid to keep it from the flames. Her eyes are a deep amber-brown, sharp and appraising—the eyes of someone who can judge the quality of metal (or character) at a glance. Her hands are calloused and scarred from countless burns and nicks, yet remain surprisingly dexterous.

She favours simple, sturdy work clothes: a heavy leather apron over a homespun tunic, thick boots, and rarely anything ornamental.

## Personality

**Traits:**
- Gruff and direct—doesn't waste words on pleasantries
- Takes immense pride in honest, quality work
- Fair in her dealings; won't cheat customers but won't be cheated either
- Patient with those willing to learn, dismissive of fools
- Quietly generous to those in genuine need

**Ideals:**
- *Craftsmanship* — "If it's worth making, it's worth making right."
- *Self-reliance* — She values those who work hard and earn their keep

**Bonds:**
- [[Willowmere]] has become her true home, more than her clan ever was
- Her forge is her sanctuary and her legacy

**Flaws:**
- Refuses to discuss her past or her clan—becomes cold and closed off if pressed
- Can be stubborn to a fault; slow to trust outsiders
- Harbours some deep regret or shame that she drowns in work

## Background

Breta left her clan in the mountains decades ago under circumstances she refuses to discuss. Some speculate she was exiled; others whisper of a personal tragedy or dishonour. Whatever the truth, she arrived in [[Willowmere]] with little more than her hammer and her skill.

Over the years, she established [[Ironfoot Smithy]] and became an indispensable part of village life. The locals long ago stopped seeing her as an outsider—to them, she's simply "the smith," as much a part of Willowmere as the old willow trees themselves.

### Rumours & Secrets

- **Rumour:** She was once a weaponsmith of some renown, crafting blades for dwarven lords or warriors of note
- **Secret:** *(GM's eyes only)* The reason she left her clan may involve a weapon she forged that was used for a terrible purpose—or perhaps a commission she refused that brought dire consequences

## Relationships

| Character | Relationship |

## Abilities & Skills

- **Master Smith:** Expert at forging and repairing tools, implements, and basic weapons
- **Weapon Crafting:** Possesses the skill to forge quality weapons, though she rarely does so now
- **Appraisal:** Can judge the quality of metalwork, ore, and weapons with a glance
- **Dwarven Resilience:** Tough and enduring, even in her later years

## Roleplaying Notes

- Speaks in short, blunt sentences; rarely uses two words when one will do
- Often continues working while talking—the rhythm of hammer strikes punctuates conversation
- Softens slightly around children or those who show genuine respect for craft
- If asked about weapons or her past skill, she deflects: *"I make what folk need. Folk here need ploughs, not swords."*

## Plot Hooks

- A traveller recognises her work—or her name—from long ago
- She needs rare ore for a special commission and asks the party to acquire it
- Someone from her clan arrives in Willowmere, seeking her out
- A local threat makes the village realise they need weapons, and Breta must confront her past as a weaponsmith
